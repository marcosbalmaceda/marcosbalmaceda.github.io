Hi! I'm Marcos, a PhD candidate in Business Economics at the Management, Strategy, and Innovation (MSI) department of KU Leuven. I will be on the 2026-2027 academic job market.

My research lies at the intersection of innovation economics, entrepreneurship, and strategy. I study how information frictions shape entrepreneurial outcomes. One stream of my work examines how emerging technologies reshape entrepreneurial opportunities, focusing on beliefs about AI and platform governance as determinants of technology adoption. A second stream investigates how technology markets and contract design affect knowledge flows and human capital retention.

Grounded in theory, I rely on econometric methods to infer causal effects and underlying mechanisms. My approach draws on both large-scale observational data and primary data collection through surveys and field experiments.

I am affiliated with the Innovation Growth Lab (IGL) Research Network.
