I am a PhD candidate in Business Economics at the Management, Strategy, and Innovation (MSI) department of KU Leuven. My advisors are Stijn Kelchtermans, Bart Leten, and Michael MÃ¶dl.

My research lies at the intersection of innovation economics, strategy, and entrepreneurship. Specifically, I examine how emerging technologies reshape entrepreneurial opportunities, and how contract design affects knowledge flows and inventor mobility.

Grounded in theory, I rely on econometrics and RCTs to identify causal effects and underlying mechanisms. My approach draws on both large-scale observational data and primary data collection through surveys and field experiments.

I held policy and research roles before starting in academia.
